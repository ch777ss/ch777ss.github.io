Skill vs Taste

2026-06-26

I had a dream recently.

There was some kind of argument happening.
I don’t remember the content clearly anymore.
But I remember my answer.

I said:

“Skills and experience are things everyone will eventually have.
What really matters in the end is taste.”

It did not feel like a conclusion.
More like something that surfaced automatically in that context.

After waking up, I kept returning to it.

It feels like there are two categories of abilities.

One category is increasingly externalizable.

Skill execution.
Workflows and operational experience.
Information retrieval and synthesis.
Standardized decision-making.
Reproducible forms of expression.

These can be written down.
Learned.
Automated.
scaled.

The other category is harder to externalize.

What you think is “good”.
What feels slightly off.
What you choose to notice.
What you trust under uncertainty.
What you decide is worth doing at all.

These are not procedures.
They behave more like an internal system of judgment.

taste = perception + preference + judgment + intuition + value system

Not just aesthetics.
More like a filtering layer on reality.

What enters attention.
What gets ignored.
What gets acted on.

A simple split emerges:

externalizable abilities → how to do things
taste → what to do, and why

As “how” becomes widely available,
“what” becomes the differentiator.

Skills converge.
Taste probably does not.
